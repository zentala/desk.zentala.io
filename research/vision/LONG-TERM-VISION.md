# Long-Term Vision: AI Health Companion

## The bigger picture

Smart Desk is not the destination. It is the entry point.

The real vision: **an AI that genuinely cares about your physical health,
is with you throughout the day, and knows you better than any doctor or trainer
who sees you once a week.**

---

## The loneliness problem nobody talks about

If you work 10 hours in front of a computer, you are essentially alone with your body
for most of your waking hours. Nobody is checking if you've moved. Nobody notices
you haven't eaten. Nobody sees your posture deteriorating at 4pm.

- Your doctor sees you once a year (if you go)
- A personal trainer is expensive and available 2h/week
- A physical therapist is for when something already broke
- Your employer doesn't care (and you don't want them to)

**Nobody is with you. AI can be.**

This is not a smart desk. This is a health companion that starts at the desk
and expands to your whole life — as much as you want it to.

---

## The progression: three products, one vision

### 1. Smart Desk (now)
*"I know when you're sitting and I'll help you stand more."*

- Sensor on desk: height (sitting/standing) + vibration (present/away)
- Mouse + keyboard: confirms you're actively working
- Electron tray app with non-annoying nudges
- Duolingo-style habit formation

### 2. Smart Move (next)
*"I know your whole day's activity and I'll help you move more."*

- Integrates with smartwatch (Apple Watch, Garmin, Wear OS)
- Steps, heart rate, sleep quality, active minutes
- Coaching: personalized, progressive, meets you where you are
- Not "you should do 10,000 steps" — "yesterday you did 4,200, let's try 4,500 today"
- Same philosophy: no judgment, positive progression, builds habit not guilt

### 3. Smart Life (future)
*"I'm with you all the time. I notice things. I help you take care of yourself."*

**The AI companion layer:**

- You wear a smartwatch 24/7 → continuous health signal stream
- Your desk knows when you work → occupational context
- AI integrates all signals → builds model of your physical state
- Proactive, not reactive: doesn't wait for you to ask, notices patterns

Examples of what it knows and does:
- "You've been sitting 8h today and your resting HR is elevated — maybe take a walk before dinner?"
- "You slept 5h last night. I'll reduce standing reminders today — recovery matters more."
- "This is your 4th week without a proper lunch break. Want to protect 12-13 on your calendar?"
- "Your activity has dropped 40% this month. Are you okay? Anything changed?"

This is not a notification app. This is a relationship.

## Smart environment: beyond the desk

If the desk can be "smart", so can other objects in your physical environment.

```
Smart Desk
    ↓
Smart Chair (posture sensor, seat time tracking)
Smart Light (circadian rhythm support, eye strain)
Smart Air (CO2 level → cognitive performance link)
Smart Move (whole-body activity)
    ↓
Smart Room (unified environment managed by AI)
    ↓
Smart Life (AI companion for physical wellbeing)
```

All of these can share the same platform, the same data model,
the same AI layer. The desk is just the first node in a network of
objects that collectively know how you're doing physically.

**AI-managed workspace** is where this goes. Not just reminders —
the environment adapts to you: lighting shifts for focus, desk rises
on schedule, air quality alert when CO2 builds up. All invisible,
all in the background, all caring.

## What we give manufacturers in the consortium

Beyond the sensor hardware, manufacturers get:

**1. UX research and insights**
- How people actually use their desks (aggregated, anonymous data)
- Which features matter and which are ignored
- What makes someone use a motorized desk daily vs. never
- Direct input into product design: "users stop using auto-raise because X"

**2. A community of practice**
- Consortium = regular meetings, shared learnings
- Competing manufacturers rarely talk — we create the neutral space
- Shared interest: grow the whole "active workplace" market
- Learn from each other's UX experiments

**3. Ecosystem positioning**
- As AI-managed environments grow, "smart desk" becomes a node
- Manufacturers who adopt the standard are positioned for that future
- Being part of Open Smart Desk Consortium = early positioning in
  the AI-managed physical environment space

**4. Distribution channel**
- Our app users are their potential customers
- "Want the full experience? Your desk needs to be compatible — here's where to buy one"
- Certified desks get recommended in the app

## The agentic layer

This is the furthest horizon — but it's worth naming now.

When you have:
- Continuous health data (smartwatch 24/7)
- Workspace context (desk sensor, computer activity)
- History (months of patterns, what works for this person)
- AI that can reason and take action

You can build **agentic health systems**:
- Not just notification → agent negotiates with your calendar to protect lunch
- Not just reminder → agent detects stress pattern and proactively suggests break
- Not just tracking → agent identifies that your productivity drops after 45min sitting
  and adjusts YOUR specific sitting threshold, not the default

This is a different category than "wellness app."
This is a personal health agent that knows you.
