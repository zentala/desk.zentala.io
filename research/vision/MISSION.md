# Mission & Philosophy

## The Problem

Millions of knowledge workers in developed economies sit 8-10 hours a day.
Height-adjustable desks were supposed to fix this. They didn't.
Most sit unused — raised once, then forgotten.

The problem was never mechanical. It was psychological.

## Why existing solutions fail

Every existing solution tries to **force** behavior change:
- Auto-raise desks: users disable after a week (annoying, intrusive)
- Timer apps (Stretchly, Stand Up!): ignored after a few days (notification fatigue)
- Smartwatch reminders: same pattern

They treat the symptom (not standing) instead of the cause (no habit, no motivation).

## Our philosophy

**The desk is not a machine. It's a psychologist.**

We don't move the desk for you. We don't nag you with alarms.
We build a system that makes you *want* to stand — through:
- Non-intrusive, context-aware nudges (no notification during meetings, calls, focus time)
- Positive reinforcement (celebrate when you do it, don't punish when you don't)
- Duolingo-style habit loops: streaks, XP, milestones, gradual progression
- A timeline that shows you your day — sitting, standing, away — so you see reality

The goal is **long-term behavior change**, not a feature you use twice.

## Mission

> Make regular position changes at work a natural, pleasant, and self-sustaining habit
> for as many knowledge workers as possible, globally.

This is a public health mission, not a product sale.
Revenue is a means to sustain the mission, not the goal.

## Why open source

- Trust: users need to know their activity data stays theirs
- Adoption: open source removes the purchase decision barrier
- Community: others can improve the algorithm, add integrations
- Standard: to become a market standard, the spec must be open

## Target users

### Primary: Remote developer
- Works from home, has a motorized desk
- Full control over their environment
- Technical enough to install and configure
- Cares about ergonomics and productivity

### Secondary: Coworking user
- No permanent desk — device travels with them
- Clips to any desk, calibrates in 30 seconds
- Personal data, never shared with coworking space

### Tertiary: Office worker (BYOD)
- Brings own device, clips to office desk
- Critical: data must be 100% personal, invisible to employer
- Privacy guarantee is the only way this group adopts

## What we are NOT building

- A surveillance tool for employers
- An automatic desk controller (removes user agency)
- A medical device (we are not doctors)
- A replacement for ergonomic consultation
