# Research & Validation Plan

## Goal

Produce credible evidence that Open Smart Desk changes behavior —
that users stand significantly more after adopting the system.

This evidence is needed to:
1. Approach manufacturers with data, not just a pitch
2. Apply for EU health/workplace wellness grants
3. Publish and gain academic credibility
4. Build user trust ("proven to work")

---

## What we need to prove

**Primary hypothesis:**
> Users of Open Smart Desk stand significantly more per day after 30 days
> of use compared to their baseline (first week).

**Secondary hypotheses:**
- App retention after 30 days is higher than typical reminder apps
- Gamification (streaks/XP) increases standing time vs. no gamification
- Non-annoying notifications cause less app uninstalls than aggressive ones

---

## Phase 1 — Self-collected data (free, now)

**What:** Anonymized aggregate data from opt-in users of the app.

**Metrics collected locally, uploaded only with explicit consent:**
- Daily sitting time (minutes)
- Daily standing time (minutes)
- Number of notifications sent vs. acted upon (response rate)
- App open rate (is user engaging with dashboard?)
- Streak length distribution

**How to present:**
- Before/after chart: week 1 vs. week 4 standing time per user
- Cohort retention: % still active after 7, 14, 30 days
- Even 50-100 users with 30-day data is a compelling story

**Privacy:**
- All data local by default
- Opt-in aggregate upload with explicit consent
- Zero PII — only behavioral metrics, no name, no IP

---

## Phase 2 — Academic partnership (low cost, high credibility)

**Target institution:** Politechnika Warszawska — Wydział Zarządzania (ergonomics)
or Akademia Wychowania Fizycznego (AWF Warszawa)

**How to approach:**
1. Cold email to 2-3 professors/lecturers with short pitch
2. Offer: free hardware kit + app access + dataset for their research
3. They design the study (academic rigor), we provide the tool
4. Win for them: real-world study, publishable data, novel topic
5. Win for us: peer-reviewed paper with our name on it

**Study format suggestion (for academic partner):**
- N=30-50 participants (enough for statistical significance for pilot)
- 8-week study: 2 weeks baseline (app installed, no gamification)
  then 6 weeks full app
- Measure: ActiGraph or similar wearable for objective activity data
  (not self-reported, which is unreliable)
- Compare: sitting time week 1-2 vs. week 7-8
- Secondary: user satisfaction survey (SUS score for app usability)

**What we provide to academic partner:**
- Free hardware kits for participants
- App with research mode (higher data granularity, opt-in)
- Technical support
- Co-authorship acknowledged in paper

## Phase 3 — EU Grants

**Relevant programs:**
- **Horizon Europe** — health and wellbeing, digital health
- **EIT Health** — European Institute of Innovation & Technology, health focus
- **NCBR (Narodowe Centrum Badań i Rozwoju)** — Polish R&D grants, requires academic partner
- **PARP** — Polish startup funding, less research-focused

**Requirements typically:**
- Polish company or academic institution as lead applicant
- Academic partner (Phase 2 relationship helps here)
- Proof of concept / prototype (we have this)
- Clear measurable health outcome (standing time, back pain reduction)

**When to apply:** After Phase 2 study is in progress or completed.
A published paper + working product = strong grant application.
